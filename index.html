The Infinite Build

This is an always-on hackathon without competitions, pitch decks, or sponsored funnels. Follow your own direction, choose your own stack.

On The Infinite Build, the builders are not the product. Everything is opt-in, fork-friendly and anti-capture.

Where we come from

Started by cryptography nerds, game designers, and hackathon regulars (and more than a few winners!) We want to go beyond weekend bursts toward ongoing collaboration. We want a place to learn cool things from each other, share as we go, and build useful things for the world.

Loose Alignment

Building can feel isolating. The Continuum is a stream to help you keep moving. Weekly get-togethers where we screen-share our CLIs and IDEs for tips and encouragement. Monthly hackathon-style weekends help our momentum. And chat's always there to ask for help (or just a friendly ear) when you need it.

(Work in progress — we’re building the place we always wished existed.)

Perpetual Progress

Fusion is a swiping game that connects builders naturally at each gathering. Choose who to learn from or work with—based on their experience or questions. The groups are formed with full privacy using MPC. No forced teams, no awkwardness, just small, introvert-friendly groups.

Fission is a group mechanism that keeps things flexible. If ideas diverge or groups grow too large, members can split constructively, forming new groups while staying connected to the original.

Together, they enable convergence and divergence, continuously opening space for collaboration and growth.

Unique Journeys

When a few people want to explore ideas or build together—say learning Rust, prototyping ZK libraries, or designing composable games—Vectors are lightweight, ephemeral groups for co-creating the experience.

Catalysts are invitations placed by builders—quick learning paths that prepare someone to journey with you. Complete one and you transform! Ready to collaborate. Everyone's encouraged to do this, to attract who they want around them. Each one is another journey starting, another group forming.
