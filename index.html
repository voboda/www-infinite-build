The Infinite Build

The Infinite Build expresses the best of hackathons—the energy, creativity, and collaboration—but in a structure for serious, long-term projects. No competitions, no pitching, no pressure. Just builders, building, at a pace that works for them.

Fundamentally Different

Most programs are extractive, built to capture developers for someone else’s goals. Rigid cycles and artificial scarcity make it clear you’re the product. Infinite Build is anti-capture: built for journey enablement, not resource control. It recognises every path is unique, shaped by the builder. Instead of channeling direction, it listens and responds. It’s built for exploration and collaboration.

Perpetual Progress

Fusion is swiping game that connects builders naturally, for a specific event. Choose who to learn from or work with—based on their experience or questions. The groups are formed with full privacy using MPC. No forced teams, no awkwardness, just small, introvert-friendly groups.

Fission is an onchain mechanism that keeps things flexible. If ideas diverge or groups grow too large, members can split constructively, forming new groups while staying connected to the original.

Together, they enable convergence and divergence, continuously opening space for collaboration and growth.

Navigating Your Path

Building can feel isolating. The Continuum offers a monthly rhythm of events to keep you moving. It’s all optional and responsive, starting with what you need. Hackathon-style weekends offer momentum. Mid-month discussions spark new ideas. Weekly get-togethers for tips and encouragement. The rest is async. A little chatbot called Harmony makes the connections you need right now.

It’s not a track or curriculum; it’s a place to find what you need, when you need it.

Unique learning journeys

No gatekeepers here. Instead, we use Beacons: clear calls to action with accessible paths. Beacons still set standards, but they invite everyone to become what’s needed to participate.

When a few people want to explore ideas or build together-say learning Rust, prototyping ZK libraries, or designing composable games-Vectors are lightweight, emphemeral DAOs. For project-based collaboration, Resonators share future success, giving builders the power-law upside of funding without the imposition or control of venture capital.
